---
title: Introducing AKT — an agent knowledge toolkit
date: 2026-06-01
excerpt: A memory layer for coding agents.
---

AKT (Agent Knowledge Toolkit) gives coding agents something they usually lack: a
memory across sessions. Before starting a non-trivial task, an agent can
**recall** relevant past work; when a chunk of work wraps up, it can **capture**
the decision that was made — and, crucially, _why_, and what was rejected.

The loop is deliberately small:

- `akt recall "<task>"` surfaces the most relevant past stories.
- `akt start-story` / `akt finish-story` record a new decision with its
  rationale and rejected alternatives.

That "decision → because → rejected alternative" shape is the whole point: it's
what makes a past choice useful the next time a similar task comes around.
